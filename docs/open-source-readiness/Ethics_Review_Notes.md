# Ethics Review Notes (Template for Medical-Adjacent Research Prototypes)

**Research Use Only. Not for clinical use.**

## Purpose

These notes are provided to help maintainers and contributors avoid avoidable harm and to support institutional ethics review discussions if the software is used in human-subject research.

## Human-Subject Research Boundary

This repository does not provide IRB/ethics approval. If you plan to use the software with human participants:
- obtain required ethics approval (IRB/REC) and local governance approvals
- perform a safety screening appropriate to audio/visual stimulation
- collect informed consent and allow participants to withdraw at any time

## Risk Areas

### Photosensitive Epilepsy (PSE)
- flicker in the 3–60 Hz range can trigger seizures in susceptible individuals
- screen participants where appropriate and provide clear stop instructions

### Discomfort and Psychological Effects
- audio/visual stimulation may cause headache, nausea, discomfort, anxiety, or sleep disruption

### Vulnerable Populations
- avoid unsupervised use with vulnerable groups without professional oversight and ethics approval

## Data Ethics

- do not collect PHI/PII
- do not publish logs from participants
- minimise data retention and prefer local-only storage

## Transparency

Any public communication must avoid clinical claims and clearly state:
- the project is not clinically validated
- results are not generalisable without controlled study design
